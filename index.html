You belong here, just as you are.

What I love most about yoga is that all you have to do is show up.

At no point in my life have I considered myself athletic. In fact, I had never even thought about taking a yoga class until I started dating my husband and he invited me to do a yoga class with him.

I walked in a little intimidated and completely out of my comfort zone, but something about that space stayed with me. It wasn't about being the strongest or most flexible person in the room. It was about breathing, being present, and learning to listen to my body in a new way.

Over time, yoga became more than just movement. It became a place of grounding, healing, and connection: a way to slow down in a world that constantly asks us to speed up.

As a teacher, my heart is to create that same kind of space for you. A space where you don't have to perform, prove, or push beyond your limits. A space where you can come as you are: whether you're brand new, returning after time away, or simply needing a moment to breathe.

You don't need to be flexible. You don't need to have it all together. You just need to show up.

And I'll meet you there.
